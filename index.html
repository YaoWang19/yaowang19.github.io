Biography

Dr. Yao Wang is an Associate Professor in the School of Electronical and Information Engineering at Zhengzhou University, currently serving as a Visiting Scholar in the School of Computer Science at the University of Auckland. He received his Ph.D. from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC) and conducted joint doctoral research at Southern Illinois University.

His research broadly encompasses hardware security and chip design. Dr. Wang specializes in digital IC and FPGA design for cryptography, with a particular focus on Physical Unclonable Functions (PUFs) and Post-Quantum Cryptography (PQC) accelerators. Furthermore, his expertise extends to mixed-signal IC design for the Internet of Things (IoT), including ultra-low-power sensor wake-up receivers and battery management ICs.

Dr. Wang's work has been published in leading academic journals such as the IEEE Internet of Things Journal and IEEE Transactions on Circuits and Systems I. His technical contributions have been recognized with the Scientific and Technological Progress Award of Henan Province. Dedicated to academic leadership and mentorship, he serves as an Executive Committee Member for the Technical Committee on Fault-Tolerant Computing of the China Computer Federation (CCF) and as a Youth Editorial Board Member for Acta Electronica Sinica.
